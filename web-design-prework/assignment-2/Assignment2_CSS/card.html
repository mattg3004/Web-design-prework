Tiger

Tigers are carnivores and eat only meat, mainly of large mammals such as deer, pigs and buffalo

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
  • Average length: 2.7m
  • Max lifespan: 16 years (in the wild)
  • Habitat: Grasslands and forests

Instantly recognizable due to their dark stripes and orange fur, tigers are the largest cat species in the world and can weigh more than 350kg. Though they are so large, they can also run quickly, up to 65km/h. Tigers used to live in Vietnam, but none have been seen here since 1997.
